Bentota Turtle Hatcheries

All visitors to Bentota enjoy visiting the Bentota Turtle Hatchery Project. The pristine golden shores that entice you to our island also offer several endangered turtle species, like the Olive Ridley and Leatherback turtles, an alluring nesting ground. The project's objective is to preserve and safeguard the eggs from predators and other threats. To find out more about these beautiful and endangered sea creatures, go to the turtle hatchery in Bentota. (Taru Villas, 2023)

Until the eggs hatch and are prepared to swim back home, the turtle hatchery guards the eggs. The project also rescues sea turtles who sustain injuries from fishing nets, motors, and other sources while at sea. Before being released back, they are frequently rehabbed. Our beachfront properties are about 10 minutes away from the Bentota Turtle Hatchery.
